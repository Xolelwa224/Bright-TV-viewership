# 📖 Project Overview

The **BrightTV Viewership Analytics** project demonstrates an end-to-end Business Intelligence workflow that transforms raw subscriber and viewing session data into actionable business insights.

Using **SQL, Databricks, Microsoft Excel, Power Query, and interactive dashboards**, this project analyzes subscriber activity, viewing behaviour, content consumption patterns, and engagement trends to support executive decision-making.

The objective is to help the CEO and Customer Value Management (CVM) team increase viewer engagement, improve customer retention, optimize content strategy, and grow BrightTV’s subscription base through data-driven recommendations.

---

# 🎯 Business Objective

The primary objective of this project is to analyze historical subscriber and viewing data and answer key business questions that support subscription growth and customer engagement.

The analysis aims to:

* Increase subscriber growth
* Improve viewer engagement
* Understand customer viewing behaviour
* Identify high-performing content
* Optimize content scheduling
* Reduce customer churn
* Support strategic business decision-making

---

# ❓ Business Questions

This analysis answers the following business questions:

* What are the overall user and viewing trends?
* Which factors influence content consumption?
* Which days experience the lowest consumption?
* What content should be promoted during low-consumption periods?
* Which customer segments are most engaged?
* How does viewing behaviour change over time?
* What initiatives can increase BrightTV’s subscription base?

# 📈 Executive Insight Summary

## Overall Platform Performance

BrightTV maintained consistent viewing activity across the analysis period, indicating stable subscriber engagement and healthy platform usage.

---

## User Engagement

The majority of viewing sessions occurred during evening hours, suggesting that subscribers primarily consume content after work and during leisure periods.

---

## Content Performance

### Highest Engagement Content

* Entertainment content generated the highest viewing time.
* Drama and series content maintained strong repeat engagement.
* Sports content showed high engagement during live events.

### Lower Engagement Content

* Educational content recorded lower viewing duration.
* Documentary content showed lower session frequency compared to entertainment categories.

---

## Consumption Trends

Daily consumption remained relatively stable, with noticeable increases during weekends and evening periods. Low-consumption periods were identified during weekday mornings and early afternoons.

---

## Viewer Behaviour

Analysis indicates that highly engaged subscribers tend to:

* Watch multiple sessions per day
* Spend longer viewing durations on entertainment content
* Return to the platform consistently during evening hours

These patterns provide opportunities for personalized recommendations and targeted retention campaigns.

---

# 💼 Business Recommendations

### Personalized Recommendations

Implement recommendation algorithms that suggest content based on viewing history and subscriber preferences.

### Low-Consumption Strategy

Promote short-form and trending entertainment content during low-consumption periods to increase platform activity.

### Retention Campaigns

Launch targeted campaigns for subscribers with declining viewing activity to reduce churn.

### Content Scheduling

Schedule premium content releases during peak evening hours to maximize engagement and viewing time.

### Loyalty Programme

Introduce rewards and loyalty initiatives for highly engaged subscribers to encourage continued platform usage.

### Marketing Initiatives

Use viewing behaviour insights to create audience-specific marketing campaigns and improve subscriber acquisition.

### Automated Reporting

Develop automated dashboards for real-time monitoring of engagement, retention, and subscription growth metrics.
